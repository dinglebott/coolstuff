Origin

The Universe is thought to have begun as a singularity, an infinitely small and dense point that contained all the matter in our Universe. The singularity then expanded rapidly in the Big Bang, becoming the Universe we know today. Learn more about the Big Bang here.

Age

Using methods such as measuring the cosmic microwave background radiation of the Universe, astronomers estimate the Universe to have existed for around 13.8 billion years. Meanwhile, its total lifespan is projected to be roughly 33 billion years.

Size

The observable universe is a sphere of radius 46.5 billion light-years around Earth. This is the region of the Universe that is theoretically visible to us. Light from outside this sphere has not had the time to reach Earth, based on the current age of the Universe. Outside this sphere, it is unknown how big the Universe really is.

Our Solar System is part of the Milky Way galaxy, which is part of a larger group of galaxies called the Local Group (about 10 million light-years long).
